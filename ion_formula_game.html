How the crisscross method works

Same charge (e.g. 1+ with 1−, or 2+ with 2−) → charges cancel 1:1. No crisscross needed.

Different charge → crisscross: each ion's charge number becomes the OTHER ion's subscript.

Worked example: Ca2+ + Cl− → charges are different (2 and 1) → cross the numbers → Ca gets subscript 1 (not written), Cl gets subscript 2 → CaCl₂
