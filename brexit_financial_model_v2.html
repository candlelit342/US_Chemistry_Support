Key Finding
The OBR estimates Brexit has permanently reduced UK GDP by ~4% vs remaining in the EU. The CER's doppelgänger model puts the gap at 5–6% by 2025, equivalent to roughly £100–130bn lost output per year.

UK–EU Trade Volumes (£bn)
Annual exports to EU and imports from EU, 2015–2025
Trade Deficit
The UK ran a £88.7bn trade deficit with the EU in 2025. Exports to the EU fell 23% from 2017–2024. The EU now accounts for 41% of UK exports and 50% of UK imports — down from 46% of exports pre-Brexit.
Brexit Trade Impact Analysis
Structural changes in UK trade composition
2016 Referendum. GBP falls 10–15%. Trade uncertainty begins.
2021 TCA in force. Non-tariff barriers introduced. UK goods exports to EU fall sharply.
2022 OBR confirms 15% long-run reduction in trade intensity vs remain.
2025 UK–EU "reset" talks ongoing; Windsor Framework stabilises NI trade.
UK Share of Global FDI (%)
UK inward FDI as % of global total
Structural Shift
The UK averaged a 3.9% share of global FDI in the eight years before Brexit. In the eight years since, the average dropped to 2.3%. In 2023–24, inward FDI turned negative.

Key Driver: Brexit-Induced Inflation
Brexit pushed up UK inflation by an estimated 1.7–2.9 percentage points through sterling depreciation and trade friction, forcing the BoE to peak at 5.25% rather than ~3.75%. The BoE's emergency cut to 0.25% in August 2016 was made explicitly due to the referendum result.
