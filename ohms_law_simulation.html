    🧮 Generalising Your Result

    You've now shown — through your own data, your own graph, and your own line of best fit — that current and voltage are directly proportional when resistance is constant. We can write this as a general equation for this circuit:

    I = Vs ÷ (Rheostat + R) = 20.0 ÷ (10 + 5) = 1.33 A
    VR (voltmeter) = I × R = 1.33 × 5 = 6.7 V

    Ideal circuit assumed: zero-resistance ammeter, rheostat wiper contact & wires. The rheostat is the series resistor used to control the current; the voltmeter reads the p.d. across the resistor under test only.
