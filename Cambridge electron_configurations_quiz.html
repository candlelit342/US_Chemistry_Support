The Hydrogen Atom Spectrum

Emission spectra, Bohr model, energy levels & series

Key Points Hydrogen produces a line spectrum – evidence that electrons occupy discrete energy levels.
Each line comes from an electron falling from a higher to a lower energy level, emitting a photon.
The ground state is n = 1 (lowest energy). The Lyman series (UV) falls to n = 1; Balmer series (visible) to n = 2; Paschen series (infrared) to n = 3.

Electron Configurations

Sub-levels, Aufbau principle, full & condensed configurations

Key Rules Maximum electrons in energy level n = 2n². Sub-levels in order: s < p < d < f.
Electrons fill from the lowest energy sub-level upward. The 4s sub-level is lower in energy than 3d.
Exceptions: Cr = [Ar] 4s1 3d5, Cu = [Ar] 4s1 3d10

Putting Electrons into Orbitals

Pauli exclusion principle, Hund's rule & orbital diagrams

Hund's rule: electrons fill degenerate orbitals singly (with parallel spins) before pairing.

Multiple Choice

The ionisation energy of hydrogen can be calculated using the emission spectrum. Which series and which feature must be used?

Worked Example 3.2 – Step-by-step

Wavelength of convergence limit = 91.46 nm = 91.46 × 10⁻⁹ m = 9.146 × 10⁻⁸ m

Step 1 → Find frequency: f = c/λ = (3.00 × 10⁸) / (9.146 × 10⁻⁸) = 3.28 × 10¹⁵ Hz

Step 2 → Find energy per photon: E = hf = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ × 3.28 × 10¹⁵ = 2.17 × 10⁻¹⁸ J

Step 3 → Multiply by Avogadro's constant: 2.17 × 10⁻¹⁸ × 6.02 × 10²³ = 1.31 × 10⁶ J mol⁻¹ = 1310 kJ mol⁻¹
