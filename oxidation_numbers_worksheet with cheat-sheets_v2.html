📖 Quick Reference — Oxidation Numbers & Redox

Rules for Assigning Oxidation Numbers

  • 1. Pure element → 0
  • 2. Monatomic ion → equals its charge
  • 3. F in compounds → always −1
  • 4. O in compounds → usually −2
    (−1 in peroxides, +2 in OF₂)
  • 5. H with non-metal → +1
    (−1 in metal hydrides e.g. NaH)
  • 6. Group 1 metals → always +1
  • 7. Group 2 metals → always +2
  • 8. Sum of all ONs = 0 (neutral) or ion charge

OIL RIG — Electron Transfer

OIL
Oxidation Is Loss of electrons
→ ON increases
→ the species is oxidised
→ it acts as a reducing agent
RIG
Reduction Is Gain of electrons
→ ON decreases
→ the species is reduced
→ it acts as an oxidising agent

⚡ Oxidation & reduction always occur together (redox).

Key Vocabulary

  • Oxidising agent — causes another to be oxidised; it is reduced
  • Reducing agent — causes another to be reduced; it is oxidised
  • Disproportionation — same element is simultaneously oxidised AND reduced
  • Half-equation — shows only the oxidation or only the reduction part of a redox reaction
  • Electron gain → reduction (ON decreases)
  • Electron loss → oxidation (ON increases)
