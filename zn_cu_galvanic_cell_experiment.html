Exploring the effect of electrode identity, aqueous ion concentration and temperature on the EMF of a cell

Interactive galvanic cell experiment: choose any two metal electrodes from magnesium, aluminium, zinc, iron, lead, copper, silver and gold for the left and right half-cells, and independently adjust each solution's concentration from 0.01 to 1.00 M and each solution's own temperature from 10 to 50°C, and read the resulting emf. A "more exotic metals" checkbox adds bismuth, tin, scandium, vanadium, chromium, manganese, nickel, uranium and plutonium to the electrode choices, with safety notes shown for the radioactive and fissile ones. Temperature shifts each electrode's standard potential according to its own empirical temperature coefficient, while the Nernst log term is evaluated at the fixed reference temperature of 298 K. A vintage mercury thermometer sits in each beaker and its column rises and falls with that beaker's own temperature slider, so only one variable changes at a time. Reversed wiring shows a negative digital reading and a needle pegged just past zero.

Standard potentials and temperature coefficients (dE°/dT) for magnesium, aluminium, zinc, iron, lead, copper and silver are literature values (Bratsch, 1989). Gold's E° is derived via Hess's law from two Bratsch couples (Au³⁺/Au⁺ and Au⁺/Au); its dE°/dT remains an approximation, since Bratsch's table gives no coefficient for the Au³⁺/Au⁺ couple.
