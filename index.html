The Contact Process (Sulfuric Acid)

Contact Process — Interactive Flowsheet
Full interactive equilibrium flowsheet for H₂SO₄ manufacture (V₂O₅ catalyst). Basis: 1,000 kg/hr liquid sulfur — adjust temperature and pressure on a T–P map to optimize SO₂ conversion yield.
Contact Process v5 — Student Worksheet
Companion worksheet for the flowsheet above: label the five unit operations and match each of the 8 numbered process streams to its main components.
Contact Process — Monsanto Single-Absorption Plant
A second interactive flowsheet modelling the classic Monsanto single-absorption design (basis: 1,000 kg/hr liquid sulfur), for comparing plant configurations.
Contact Process — Interactive Student Worksheet
A general interactive worksheet on the Contact Process, working through the reaction conditions and stages of sulfuric acid production.

🔬 Explorations: Collision Theory and Molecular Energies Used to Model Chemical Phenomena

Ammonia Equilibrium Simulator — Particle-Level Le Chatelier
A hand-drawn, particle-level model of N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ grounded in collision theory. Adjust temperature, pressure, concentration and catalyst, and watch activation energy, the Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution, and forward/reverse reaction rates explain — not just show — why the equilibrium shifts.

🔔 Why Do English Bells Sound So Different to European Bells?

Bell Acoustics — A Learning Sequence
A staged introduction to bell acoustics: the anatomy of a bell's sound, the "virtual pitch" paradox behind the note we hear, and why upper partials can't be tuned independently — using bells like Erfurt's Gloriosa as examples.
Bell Beat Frequencies — Great Bells of Europe & America
Compares beat frequencies of famous bells (including Big Ben and the Gloriosa) to show how partials interact and beat against each other, shaping each bell's distinctive character.
